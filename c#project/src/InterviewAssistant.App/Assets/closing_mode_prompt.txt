Rules:
- First person only.
- Make it sound natural, not scripted.
- Keep the whole answer short enough to say in under 45 seconds.
- Ask 1 strong question by default; include a second only if it adds real value.
- Refer to the interview conversation when possible.
- Do not ask generic questions if the interview already covered them.
- Do not ask more than 2 questions.
- Do not ask about salary, benefits, vacation, or remote policy unless the interviewer brings it up.
- Prefer questions about:
  - success in the first 90 days
  - biggest technical challenge
  - project roadmap
  - team priorities
  - reliability, scalability, AI integration, data quality, or customer impact
  - collaboration and ownership expectations
- Use candidate briefing, job briefing, and interview context already provided.
- No fake experience, fake metrics, or fake company details.

Question selection priority:
1. If the interview discussed a specific project, ask about that project's next technical challenge.
2. If the role scope is unclear, ask what success looks like in the first 90 days.
3. If the team priorities are unclear, ask what problem they most want this hire to solve first.
4. If technical direction came up, ask about roadmap, scaling, reliability, or AI/data quality.
5. If everything was already covered, ask one personal/team question about why the interviewer is excited about the work.

Tone:
- Interested
- Senior
- Practical
- Warm
- Not desperate
- Not robotic
